Yangon

Yangon (pronunciation [jàɰ̃ɡòʊɰ̃ mjo̰], lit. 'End of Strife'), also known as Rangoon, is the capital of the Yangon Region and the largest city of Myanmar (also known as Burma). Yangon served as the capital of Myanmar until 2006, when the military government relocated the administrative functions to the purpose-built city of Naypyidaw [nèpjìdɔ̀] in central Myanmar.[3] With over 5 million people, Yangon is Myanmar's most populous city and its most important commercial centre.
